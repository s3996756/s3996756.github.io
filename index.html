Interactive Experience Research Project

For this assignment, I will be writting in response to my experience with Min Jea Seo's interactive web project.

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

When first engaging with the webpage, my attention was captured by the vibrant comic book-style layout. The bright colours and playful animations instantly made it feel like I was entering into the pages of a story. This design choice not only made the webpage visually appealing but also gave it a unique personality that made me want to explore further.

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • ‘Resume’ and ‘Linked in’ button change to a lighter yellow when hovering over them
  • As I scroll and hover over different sections, textboxes with headings and body text pop-up. These disappear when the mouse leaves that section
  • The arrow icon in the top left corner of the textboxes moves when you hover over it
  • Textboxes slightly change to lighter grey when directly hovering over them
  • The white and grey panels, the text changes depending on the direction you scroll
  • The items under the articles and projects lists change to purple when hovering over them
  • ‘Email’ and ‘Linked in’ and hamburger menu button all change when hovering directly over

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time engaging with the comic book-style panels in the main body of the webpage. I was drawn to them because of their colourful design and animations. What interested me the most was how the imagery provided a glimpse into the content before even interacting with it. This feature made the browsing experience more engaging and interactive. I found myself spending extra time hovering over each panel, and comparing it to the imagery underneath .

What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action was definitely the textboxes popping up. Some of the other actions like the hover effect on the buttons are a lot more discrete since the hover area that triggers the action is smaller. The textboxes popping up as you scroll is the most common since the area of the panels is quite large.

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

Considering this webpage is a designer's portfolio, the primary goal is to showcase the designer's UX design skills. The interactive elements, such as the comic book-style layout, engaging animations and navigation, serve as a demonstration of the designer's creativity and ability to create an immersive user experience

How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

  • Visual design: the vibrant and attractive design layout quickly grabs the users attention and encourages them to keep exploring. This also allows him to showcase his creativity and unique approaches to problems.
  • Engaging interactions: the interactive elements, such as the hover effects and animated transitions, enhance the user experience and encourage users to further explore the website.
  • Presentation: the presentation of the content effectively communicates the designer’s storytelling ability and provides a narrative that guides the user through the portfolio.
  • Navigation: the navigation on the website is intuitive and easy to use. This demonstrates their understanding of design principles and mental models.

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

This page is designed to be easily interacted with in a small amount of time (2-5 minutes). This is logical given it is a portfolio and potential employers or clients might not spend a lot of time reading lengthy webpages. By offering sufficient context and prominent links to important content, users can quickly access the most relevant information without the need for extensive browsing or visiting multiple times.

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

Users are drawn in by the engaging design and concise presentation of information, making it easy to quickly grasp the essence of the portfolio. As users explore further, they encounter interactive elements and details that invite them to delve further into specific projects or sections. The seamless navigation guides users through the portfolio, making it easy to access content and information during the first visit.

What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The strongest reference within this experience is its reference to comic books. Bold animations, text and visual layout are utilised to enhance the visual storytelling. Being the primary visual style, it adds a playful and engaging element to the webpage.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

The reference to comic books communicates that when engaging with the experience, I should act with a sense of exploration, interaction, and attentiveness. I should actively pay attention to detail and explore different sections. Additionally, I should pay close attention to the storytelling elements present in the portfolio, piecing together information and themes.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

I should approach the experience with a sense of curiosity, ready to explore their creation. The interactive elements and storytelling aspects of comic books suggest that I should be actively engaged and open to uncovering hidden details and narratives within the pages. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

I haven't encountered any significant frustrations while interacting with the webpage. This lack of frustration seems to stem from the thoughtful consideration the creator put into the user experience. Their attention to detail likely ensured smooth navigation and easy access to information, minimising potential points of frustration for users. The only minor challenge I encountered was with the heading that changes based on scroll direction. Initially, I didn't notice this feature immediately, and when I did, I found it slightly difficult to interact with smoothly, especially when using a trackpad.

What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part of the interaction for me is the seamless navigation and the visually captivating design of the webpage. The intuitive layout and smooth transitions between sections make it effortless to explore the content, while the vibrant colours and dynamic animations add a sense of excitement and engagement.
